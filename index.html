How Capacity Planning Works in Workforce Management

Step 1: Workload Calculation

Convert volume and AHT into total workload hours.

Step 2: Available Capacity

Adjust paid hours using shrinkage and occupancy.

Step 3: Gap Analysis

Compare required FTE vs current headcount.

Step 4: Hiring Plan

Apply attrition and ramp curves to project hiring needs.

Key Capacity Planning Terms

FTE (Full-Time Equivalent)
Number of agents required to handle workload.
Shrinkage
Time agents are paid but unavailable.
Occupancy
Percentage of time agents are actively working.
Attrition
Rate at which employees leave.
Ramp-Up
Time taken for new hires to reach full productivity.

Core Capacity Planning Formulas

Workload Hours = (Volume × AHT) / 3600
Required FTE = Workload Hours / (Paid Hours × (1 - Shrinkage) × Occupancy)
Net FTE Gap = Required FTE - Current Headcount

Capacity Planning FAQs

How do you calculate required FTE in a call center?

Required FTE is calculated using workload, shrinkage, and occupancy assumptions.

What is shrinkage in workforce planning?

Shrinkage is the percentage of paid time not spent handling work.

How does attrition impact hiring plans?

Higher attrition increases hiring requirements to maintain staffing levels.

What is a good occupancy rate?

Typically 80–90% depending on service levels.
